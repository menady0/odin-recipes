Chicken Stir-Fry Recipe

Description

Chicken stir-fry is a fast, vibrant, and highly nutritious dish that combines tender pieces of chicken breast with a colorful assortment of crisp vegetables. Rooted in traditional Asian cooking techniques, it relies on high-heat cooking to quickly sear the ingredients while locking in their natural juices, colors, and nutrients. The entire dish is brought together by a savory, slightly sweet sauce that coats every element beautifully. This recipe is incredibly popular for weeknight dinners because it requires minimal cooking time once the prep work is finished. It serves as an excellent canvas for whatever vegetables you have on hand in the refrigerator, making it both economical and adaptable. Typically served over a bed of fluffy rice or tossed with noodles, it delivers a satisfying, well-balanced meal in minutes.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breasts (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • Soy sauce (low sodium preferred)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Fresh ginger (grated)
  • Sesame oil
  • Vegetable oil (or canola oil for frying)
  • Broccoli florets
  • Bell peppers (sliced)
  • Carrots (thinly sliced)
  • Snap peas or snow peas
  • Cornstarch (for thickening the sauce)
  • Honey or brown sugar
  • Chicken broth
  • Green onions (sliced, for garnish)
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken: Toss the chicken pieces with a tablespoon of soy sauce and a teaspoon of sesame oil. Let it sit while you prep the vegetables.
  2. Whisk the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining soy sauce, chicken broth, honey, minced garlic, grated ginger, and cornstarch until smooth.
  3. Sear the chicken: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned and cooked through, then remove it from the pan and set aside.
  4. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add a bit more oil to the same pan. Toss in the broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, and snap peas. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until tender-crisp.
  5. Combine ingredients: Return the cooked chicken to the skillet with the vegetables and turn the heat down to medium.
  6. Thicken the sauce: Give your sauce mixture a quick stir and pour it into the skillet. Cook, stirring constantly, for 1–2 minutes until the sauce bubbles and thickens.
  7. Garnish: Remove from heat and top with sliced green onions and sesame seeds if desired.
  8. Serve hot: Dish out immediately over a warm bed of white rice, brown rice, or noodles.
