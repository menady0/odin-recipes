Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked pasta dish made by layering wide sheets of pasta with rich fillings such as meat sauce, bechamel, cheese, and sometimes vegetables. It is then baked until the top becomes golden and bubbling, creating a comforting combination of soft pasta, creamy sauce, and savory filling in every slice. Each region and household tends to have its own variation, but the core idea remains the same: layered flavors that meld together during baking. What makes lasagna especially popular is its balance of textures and its ability to be prepared ahead of time. The layers hold up well after resting, which allows the flavors to deepen even more when reheated. Whether made with a slow-cooked ragù, a simple tomato sauce, or a vegetarian filling like spinach and ricotta, lasagna is widely appreciated as a hearty, crowd-pleasing dish suitable for family meals and gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna sheets (pasta sheets)
  • Ground beef or ground pork (or a mix)
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste (optional, for richer flavor)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Dried herbs (such as oregano and basil)
  • Ricotta cheese (or béchamel sauce)
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • Parmesan cheese (grated)
  • Mushrooms (optional)
  • Spinach (optional)
  • Carrots (optional, finely chopped)
  • Milk (if making béchamel sauce)
  • Red wine (optional, for sauce depth)

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat sauce: Heat olive oil in a large pan. Sauté the chopped onion (and carrots, if using) until soft. Add minced garlic and cook briefly until fragrant.
  2. Cook the meat: Add ground beef or pork (or a mix). Cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks. Season with salt, black pepper, oregano, and basil.
  3. Build the sauce: Stir in tomato paste (if using), then add tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes. Add a splash of red wine (optional) for depth. Simmer for 20–30 minutes. Add mushrooms or spinach if you want them in the sauce.
  4. Prepare the cheese layer: If using ricotta, mix it with a little salt and pepper. If using béchamel, warm milk and prepare a smooth white sauce with butter and flour.
  5. Pre-cook or soften pasta (if needed): Check your lasagna sheets instructions—some require boiling, others are oven-ready.
  6. Assemble the lasagna: In a baking dish, layer in this order: meat sauce → lasagna sheets → ricotta or béchamel → mozzarella → repeat until ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and a generous layer of mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  7. Bake: Cover with foil and bake at about 180°C (350°F) for 25–30 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 10–15 minutes until the top is golden and bubbling.
  8. Rest before serving: Let it sit for 10–15 minutes so it sets and slices cleanly.
