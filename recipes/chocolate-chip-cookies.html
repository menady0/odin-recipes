Chocolate Chip Cookies Recipe

Description

Chocolate chip cookies are a quintessential baked treat loved worldwide for their perfect balance of sweet, buttery dough and rich chocolate pockets. Characterized by crisp edges and a soft, chewy center, these cookies offer a nostalgic comfort that pairs beautifully with a glass of cold milk. The secret to their incredible flavor profile lies in the combination of white sugar for crispiness and brown sugar for a deep, caramel-like chewiness. What makes this recipe a timeless favorite is its versatility and simplicity. You can easily customize the cookies by altering the size of the chocolate chunks, adding a pinch of sea salt on top, or chilling the dough to intensify the flavors. Whether baked fresh for an afternoon snack or prepared for a holiday gathering, these cookies are a reliable crowd-pleaser that fills the kitchen with an irresistible aroma.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Unsalted butter (softened)
  • Granulated white sugar
  • Brown sugar (packed)
  • Vanilla extract
  • Eggs
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chips (or chunks)
  • Walnuts or pecans (optional, chopped)

Steps

  1. Preheat and prep: Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F) and line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Cream butter and sugars: In a large bowl, beat the softened unsalted butter, white sugar, and packed brown sugar together until the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  4. Add wet ingredients: Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract until fully incorporated.
  5. Combine wet and dry: Gradually blend the dry flour mixture into the wet creamed ingredients until a soft dough forms. Do not overmix.
  6. Fold in extras: Gently fold in the semi-sweet chocolate chips (and chopped nuts, if using) using a wooden spoon or spatula.
  7. Bake: Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving space between them. Bake for 9–11 minutes until the edges are golden brown.
  8. Cool before serving: Let the cookies sit on the baking sheet for 2 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
